Large Israeli force sweeps into Nablus
Hundreds of Israeli soldiers backed by as many as 150 armored vehicles blitzed into this besieged West Bank town Friday and put a noose around the Old City, searching for Palestinian fighters and groups connected with recent attacks in Israel, including Wednesday's blast at Jerusalem's Hebrew University, which killed five Americans.
John Ward Anderson
NEWS ANALYSIS As companies fail, Bush pulls out handcuffs
The WorldCom case now joins the parade of high-profile indictments, complaints and investigations that have quickly become the vital center of the administration's strategy for reducing political vulnerability for the White House. The administration of President George W. Bush long relished the close ties to the corporate world that it is now straining to shun.
Stephen Labaton
Still a minority, a new black elite prospers in South Africa
For generations, the stately Pietersburg Golf Club here has been a symbol of white power and prestige, the place where businessmen cut deals between sips of whiskey while their sleek Mercedes-Benzes sat in the parking lot. But on this day, most of the golfers are black men.
Rachel L. Swarns
