 	 ALLIES CONTINUE TO PUSH NORTHWARD 	
British soldiers fired 105mm guns at Iraqi positions to assist the assault on the Al-Faw peninsula, in southern Iraq, on Saturday. More Coverage  AP Photo/Stephen Hird, Pool 	
 
U.S. pounds Baghdad with bombs	 
The United States began a blistering series of bombing attacks on Baghdad and other Iraqi cities after dusk Friday, destroying entire rows of buildings and leaving parts of the city ablaze. 
Thomas Fuller/IHT
Iraqi regime is losing control, Rumsfeld says	 
The Iraqi "regime is starting to lose control of their country," Defense Secretary Donald Rumsfeld asserted Friday afternoon.
Joel Brinkley/NYT
Joy, muted by memories of the last time	 
As hundreds of troops swept into Safwan just after dawn, the heartache of a town that has felt some of the hardest edges of Saddam Hussein’s rule seemed to burst forth.
Dexter Filkins/NYT
Armored brigades charge across desert	 
U.S. armored and infantry brigades swept across the desolate desert here Friday, seizing an abandoned airstrip at Jalabah and advancing to the edge of the Euphrates River Valley. 
Steven Lee Myers/NYT
In Mideast, in Midwest, anti-war protests erupt	 
Protests against the war in Iraq continued in cities around the globe Friday, many of them erupting spontaneously and bringing disruption to crowded urban areas.
Brian Knowlton/IHT
Blair-Chirac quarrel rages on at EU summit	 
The battle within Europe raged on as President Jacques Chirac of France vowed Friday to oppose a British idea for a Security Council resolution that would give the United States and Britain the right to govern Iraq.
Elaine Sciolino/NYT
