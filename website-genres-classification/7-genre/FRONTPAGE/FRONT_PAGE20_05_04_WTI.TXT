    U.S. strike on house in desert kills 40	       
    	      
                                        
             
 	           
                      (AP)
        	      
      A U.S. aircraft fired on a house in the desert near the Syrian border yesterday, and Iraqi officials said more than 40 people were killed, including 15 children. U.S. military officials said the target was a suspected safe house for foreign fighters from Syria, but Iraqis said a helicopter had attacked a wedding party.	      
    
Anglicans seek gay bishop removal
    Eighteen Anglican archbishops, most of them from Africa and Asia and representing more than 55 million Anglicans, have called on the Episcopal Church to "repent" its pro-homosexual policies within three months or face expulsion from the worldwide Anglican Communion.	        
   House approves one-year budget deal
    he House last night passed a one-year budget agreement that makes cutting taxes easier than increasing spending, daring the Senate to block final passage and directly challenging a few Senate Republicans who want to make it harder to pass tax cuts.	        
   Israel fires missile at Palestinian rally
    Israeli forces fired tank shells and a missile on Palestinian demonstrators in the southern Gaza city of Rafah yesterday, killing at least 10 persons, including children, and wounding more than 50.	        
   U.N. auditors fault oil-for-food monitor
    The company hired to monitor aid under the U.N. oil-for-food program in Iraq failed to staff key checkpoints, used unauthorized subcontractors, and could not account for "massive discrepancies" between reported and actual shipments of aid, according to an internal U.N. audit.
