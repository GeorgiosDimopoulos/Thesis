Kerry wins in five states	      
    	      
                  
            
 	        
            Democratic presidential hopeful Sen. John Kerry, D-Mass., cheers Tuesday night in Seattle.
             (AP)
          	         
      Sen. John Edwards solidly won the South Carolina Democratic primary and Wesley Clark claimed victory in Oklahoma, but Sen. John Kerry easily triumphed in five other states in an impressive showing last night.    	     
     
Lieberman quits; Clark wins Oklahoma	      
    	      
                                        
              
 	          
                       (AP)
       	      
      Sen. Joe Lieberman ended his presidential bid last night after failing to win any of the first 10 primaries and caucuses, and Wesley Clark proclaimed his lone victory in Oklahoma yesterday will keep him in the race to challenge President Bush in November.	     
           
    GOP lawmakers plan cuts in Bush budget	      
    	      
                     Congressional Republicans, in an extraordinary break with the White House in an election year, say President Bush's 2005 budget proposal "doesn't go far enough" to restrain government spending and are considering pursuing further cuts in outlays.	     
    
Ricin find closes 3 Senate buildings
    Federal authorities shut down three Senate office buildings yesterday after it was confirmed that the deadly poison ricin was found in Senate Majority Leader Bill Frist's office on Monday &#8212; the second toxic attack on the U.S. Capitol in 21/2 years.	       
   White House seeks to loan U.N. funds for renovations
    The Bush administration's new budget includes a $1.2 billion, 30-year loan to renovate the aging United Nations headquarters and build a new annex, although U.N. officials expressed disappointment that Washington will charge interest on the loan.	       
   Builders in Iraq must factor in cost of security
    U.S. companies bidding for massive reconstruction contracts in Iraq will spend more than $1 billion and employ up to 200,000 people to protect their staff and work sites, consultants say.	       
   Faculty leaders at UDC call for Pollard's ouster
    Faculty leaders at the University of the District of Columbia are pushing for the removal of the school's president, citing lost confidence in his leadership and continued resentment over his hiring of a family friend as provost.
