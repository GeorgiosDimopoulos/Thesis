U.S. advisers see Saudis as enemies
A recent briefing given to a top Pentagon advisory board described Saudi Arabia as an enemy of the United States, and recommended that U.S. officials give it an ultimatum to stop backing terrorism or face seizure of its oil fields and its financial assets invested in the United States.
Thomas E. Ricks
The reason to cry for Argentina
Until last year, Argentines were part of the richest, best-educated and most cultured nation in Latin America. The poor here lived with more dignity than their equals anywhere else in the region. Argentina was, as the Argentines liked to say, very civilized. Not anymore. 
Anthony Faiola
The dollar rebounds, but for how long?
The dollar, in retreat for much of this year, is making a tentative comeback, helped by its allure as a bulwark of safety in turbulent times. Though analysts say the danger of a renewed recession in the United States is growing, potential political and financial hazards outside America are piling up even faster. 
Eric Pfanner
Democrats' hopes rise with economic volatility
Rising voter concern about the future of the United States, driven by an unsteady economy and unrest on Wall Street, is stirring nervousness among some Republicans and lifting confidence among Democratic leaders that their party could capture control of the House in November.
Adam Nagourney
Free coins in the fountain
For countless tourists over two and a half centuries, the Trevi Fountain has been a source of awe and a wellspring of hope, promising another visit to the Eternal City, and a wish fulfilled, to anyone who tosses a coin into its gurgling waters. For Roberto Cercelletta, it has been a lucrative pool of clandestine profit. What tourists gave, Cercelletta took away. 
Frank Bruni
